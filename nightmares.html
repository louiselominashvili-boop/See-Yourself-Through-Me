Dreams: The Dialogue Between Fear and Freedom

Dreams are more than passing illusions of the night; they are the language of the subconscious, a bridge between what we know and what we avoid. Some believe that when nightmares come, it is not random chaos, but a test — as if demons are playing with us. Yet what these “demons” truly seek is not our destruction, but our fear. Fear is the energy they feed upon.

The secret, then, is laughter. Laughter is not merely a reaction; it is a vibration of freedom. When you laugh in the face of fear, the nightmare dissolves, and what once terrified you loses its grip. It is said that archangels have the best sense of humor, and perhaps that is why lower vibrational forces tremble before them — not because of their power, but because of their joy. Joy is untouchable by darkness.

Dreams are feelings that you have “eaten” during the day — emotions consumed but not digested. If they remain stuck inside you, they ferment into chaos, waiting to burst. Your subconscious, kind and patient, helps you process these leftovers while you sleep. Nightmares, then, are the shadows of your unprocessed emotions — the fears, the regrets, the grief that long to be acknowledged. They come not to harm you, but to ask for recognition.

If you do not wish for these feelings to rise as monsters in your sleep, you must face them in waking life. You must decide: will you live like a deer, running from every rustle in the dark, or like a snake, shedding your old skin to grow anew? The choice is yours — the dreamer’s choice.

One way to begin is by listening to yourself — truly listening. Record your thoughts, your feelings, even your pain, and play them back as if they belong to someone else. Then, become the helper instead of the judge. See where your own code has bugs, and gently fix them. The goal is not perfection, but understanding.

Remember, the brain is just a computer. It will believe any code you give it. Feed it fear, and it will build prisons. Feed it love and laughter, and it will create wings. The choice of what you believe — and therefore what you become — lies within your hands.
